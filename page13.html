Costume Design

Carmen… una y otra vez
Context

This project was created with the purpose of supporting cultural activities within the country of El Salvador, it was coordinated by the French Alliance (located in San Salvador) and the Presidency’s Secretariat of Culture through the Department of Contemporary Dance of the School “Morena Celarié”.

It consisted in adapting the French play "Carmen" by Prosper Mérimée to the Salvadoran reality and for its elaboration various universities and national institutes participated. 

Finally, the contemporary dance show “Carmen… una y otra vez” had its world premiere at the National Theater with live music by the jazz ensemble of the National Arts Center (CENAR) performed by artists from the School “Morena Celarié”.

Mandate and process
On our school’s behalf, more than 50 students were commissioned with creating costume proposals for the play. A workshop was held at the facilities of the "Morena Celarié" school where the choreography of the dance was presented to the students, the dancers were also interviewed to find out their needs and discuss their vision of the character they will be interpreting.

Designs

As the main decoration for the costumes, I used faces that represented the people that make up Salvadoran society, this because the purpose of the adaptation was to show that the events that take place in the original play happen over
and over again within different societies.

The designs were loose and open or fitted and ending at the knee to accommodate the demands and
movements of the dancers. 

Specifics

Townspeople: earth tones were used to blend them within the background but were also mixed with a few bright colors to represent the movement of the society.

Carmen: representing the Salvadoran woman, shows three stages during the play:
-Passionate and in control, therefore burgundy was used to represent her wild side but was grounded with the use of black and white to give her a more elegant look.
-Dreamy and filled with illusion, for this design purple and yellow were used to represent her fantasies and a see-through fabric to evoque the lightness of a what a dream is.
-Her last stage was abused, suffering but strong before her trials, so black and white was used as representation of the death of both her dreams and as a reality she sometimes faces with a combination of a bit of red to represent the violence she goes through.

The boyfriend: since he is a cheater, a vest made out of 2 faces was created for him, this represents both sides of his persona, the one he shows to Carmen and the one he shows society.

The military man: squares were used to represent camouflage on a military style jacket. 

Once the designs were presented, it was left to the dance school and the dancers to choose which one would be used for the play. Of the submitted designs, four were selected and made by Paula de Pérez and Joaquín Salazar.
